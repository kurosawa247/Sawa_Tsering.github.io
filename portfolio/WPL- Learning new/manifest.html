Evidence title: WPL: Learning new

Evidence description:

During my internship, I realized how important it is to keep learning continuously in the IT sector. I worked with different technologies and tools such as Proxmox, Docker, NetBox, ArubaOS, Windows Server, backup strategies, and network segmentation. I also had to adapt to different management interfaces, such as the GUI of an Aruba Instant On switch. This showed me that an IT professional must not only apply existing knowledge, but also keep learning new things to stay current.

I actively looked for documentation, manuals, and technical explanations whenever I encountered something new or needed to solve a problem. By using official documentation, technical information, and practical examples, I could understand systems faster and configure them correctly. This helped me stay up to date and steadily expand my knowledge.

I also learned the value of tutorials, guidance from colleagues, and hands-on practice. In a real work environment, a lot of learning happens by trying things, making mistakes, and improving your approach afterward. This internship made it clear to me that lifelong learning is not an extra task in IT, but a necessary part of the profession.

What I learned

  • How to stay up to date with new technologies and systems.

  • How to use documentation and manuals to learn new skills.

  • How to find and apply technical information efficiently.

  • How to learn from tutorials, colleagues, and practical experience.

  • How to adapt to new interfaces and tools quickly.

  • Why continuous learning is essential in IT.
